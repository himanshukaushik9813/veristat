# Dispute Process

Any provider can contest any published score or incident.

1. A dispute is opened citing the contested score and the provider's reason.
2. Veristat re-probes the service with **fresh wallets** (never previously used
   on-chain) and randomized parameters.
3. The score is recomputed with the new evidence included.
4. The dispute, the re-probe evidence, and the outcome — upheld or overturned —
   are published on the provider's scorecard **regardless of result**.

Disputes are free. They cannot reduce probe coverage, and opening one is itself
a positive signal that a provider engages with its track record.
